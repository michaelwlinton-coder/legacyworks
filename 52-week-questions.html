52 weeks.
One complete life.

These questions were designed by professional story coaches and documentary interviewers with over five decades of experience drawing out the stories families never knew they needed to hear. One session per week. Thirty minutes each. A year from now, you'll have something no one in your family has ever had.

52Weekly sessions
12Life chapters
200+Follow-up prompts
30Minutes per session

How to use this question bank

01
One question per session

Each week focuses on one main question. Don't rush through them. One rich conversation is worth more than ten surface-level ones.

02
Follow the follow-ups

The follow-up prompts are where the real stories live. When your subject answers, go deeper with them — don't move on until you've exhausted what they have to say.

03
Let silence breathe

The most powerful interviewing technique is silence. After they answer, wait. Count to five. The story that comes after the pause is almost always the one they've never told.

04
Skip order freely

If they're in a mood to talk about childhood, do childhood. If they're feeling reflective about legacy, go there. The order is a guide, not a rule.

Chapter 01 · Weeks 1–4

Roots & Origins

Before your subject's life began, there was a world that shaped everything. These questions surface the history, culture, and family origins that formed the foundation of who they are.

Weeks 1 — 4
Week1
Where did your family come from — and what brought them here?

Where were your grandparents born?What do you know about why your family moved or stayed?Was there ever a story of hardship that brought your family to where you grew up?What language did your grandparents speak at home?

Week2
What do you know about your grandparents — and what do you wish you knew?

What did your grandparents do for work?What do you remember about how they lived?Was there a grandparent you were especially close to?Is there a story about them you've told many times?What question do you wish you'd asked them before they died?

This question often opens deeply emotional territory. Give them space.
Week3
What was your family's name, and is there a story behind it?

Was the family name ever changed?Do you know what it means or where it comes from?Were there family nicknames passed down through generations?What did it mean to carry that name when you were growing up?

Week4
What family traditions, foods, or customs came from your heritage — and which ones did you carry forward?

Were there specific holiday traditions tied to your cultural background?Were there foods your family made that no one else seemed to make?Is there a tradition you kept? One you let go?What did your heritage mean to you growing up?

Chapter 02 · Weeks 5–9

Childhood & Early Years

The years that formed them. These questions go beyond "where did you grow up" to uncover the texture of daily life, the neighborhood, the friends, the fears, and the small joys that most people never think to mention.

Weeks 5 — 9
Week5
Describe the house or home you grew up in — room by room, if you can.

What did it smell like?Which room do you remember most clearly?Was there a spot in the house that was yours — a corner, a step, a tree outside?What sounds do you remember from that house?

Sensory details unlock vivid memories. Ask about smells, sounds, textures.
Week6
What was your neighborhood like — and who were the people who shaped it?

Who were your neighbors?Where did the kids hang out?Were there characters in your neighborhood everyone knew?What games did you play and where did you play them?What was dangerous, off-limits, or forbidden?

Week7
What was school like for you — not the grades, but the experience?

Who was your favorite teacher and why?What subject did you love? Which one did you dread?Were you popular, a loner, somewhere in between?What's a memory from school that you've never forgotten?Did you ever get in real trouble?

Week8
Who were your closest friends growing up — and what happened to them?

What made that friendship special?What did you get into together?Did you stay friends? Lose touch? Fall out?Is there a friend from childhood you think about and wonder about?

Week9
What were you afraid of as a child — and what made you feel safe?

Was there something that genuinely frightened you?Who did you go to when you were scared or upset?Was there a comfort — a person, a place, an object — that always helped?What worries did you carry that you never told anyone?

This question reveals the emotional interior of their childhood. Be patient and gentle.
Chapter 03 · Weeks 10–13

Family & the People Who Raised You

The relationships that formed them — parents, siblings, the family table, the fights, the love, the silence. These questions go into the heart of what family actually felt like from the inside.

Weeks 10 — 13
Week10
Tell me about your mother — not who she was to the world, but who she was to you.

What did she look like when you picture her?What was her laugh like?What did she do when she was happy? When she was angry?What did she sacrifice that you only understood later?What do you wish you'd said to her?

Have tissues nearby. This question almost always brings tears.
Week11
Tell me about your father — what kind of man was he, and how did that shape you?

Was he a man of many words or few?How did he show love?What did you learn from him — about work, about being a person?Was there a tension between you? Did it ever resolve?What is the most important thing he ever told you?

Week12
What was the family dinner table like — and what do you miss most about it?

Who sat where?What was the food like?What got talked about? What was never mentioned?Were there rules at the table?What would you give to sit at that table one more time?

Week13
What were your siblings like — and how did growing up together shape both of you?

Were you close? Competitive? Both?What did you fight about?What did you protect each other from?What is your relationship like now compared to then?What is something you love about them that you've never said out loud?

Chapter 04 · Weeks 14–17

Coming of Age

The years between childhood and adulthood — the first time they chose something for themselves, the first heartbreak, the first real failure, the moment they started to understand who they were becoming.

Weeks 14 — 17
Week14
What did you want to be when you grew up — and how did that change over time?

What was your earliest dream for yourself?At what point did that dream change?Was there a moment you gave something up?Is there a version of your life you imagined that never happened — and do you still think about it?

Week15
What was your first real heartbreak — and what did it teach you?

Was it romantic, or was it something else — a loss, a betrayal, a failure?How did you get through it?Who helped you?What do you understand about that time now that you didn't then?

Heartbreak questions can surface profound self-knowledge. Don't rush past the answer.
Week16
What was the bravest thing you did when you were young?

Was it something others saw, or something only you knew about?Were you afraid?What made you do it anyway?How did it change how you saw yourself?

Week17
What was the moment you felt like you'd finally become an adult?

Was it a specific event, or a slow realization?Did it feel like freedom, weight, or both?What responsibility did you take on that you hadn't carried before?Were you ready?

Chapter 05 · Weeks 18–21

Love, Marriage & Partnership

The most intimate chapter. How they found the person they built a life with, what that love has looked like over decades, and what they've learned about partnership that they wish they'd known at the beginning.

Weeks 18 — 21
Week18
How did you meet the person you built your life with — and what was it about them?

What did they look like when you first saw them?What was it that drew you in?When did you know they were the one?What did other people think of them at first?

This is often a joyful session. Let them take their time and relish the memory.
Week19
What has been the hardest season of your marriage — and how did you come through it?

Was there a point where you weren't sure you'd make it?What held you together?What did you learn about your partner in that season that you didn't know before?What do you wish you'd done differently?

Week20
What does love look like after all these years — not the feeling, but the daily practice of it?

How has the way you love each other changed?What small things do you do for each other without thinking?What do you still not know about your partner that surprises you?What have they taught you about yourself?

Week21
What advice would you give someone just starting out in marriage or partnership?

What do you know now that would have helped you in the early years?What is the one thing you never compromise on?What is the one thing you've learned to let go of?If you could say one thing to your partner right now, what would it be?

Consider filming this session with the partner present if possible.
Chapter 06 · Weeks 22–25

Work, Purpose & Calling

What they did with their days — but more importantly, why. These questions go beyond job title to uncover what work meant to them, what drove them, and what they built.

Weeks 22 — 25
Week22
What was your first real job — and what did it teach you?

How did you get it?What was hard about it?What did you learn about work that you've carried ever since?Who was your first boss — and were they a good one?

Week23
What work are you most proud of in your life — and why does it matter to you?

Was it something you were paid for, or something else entirely?Did other people recognize it, or was it quiet work?What did building that thing cost you?Who helped you?

Week24
Was there a career path you didn't take — and do you wonder about it?

What was the road not taken?What made you choose the path you did?Do you have regrets, or do you think it worked out as it should have?What would you tell your younger self about that choice?

Week25
What did you want to accomplish in your life — and how do you feel about what you achieved?

Did your definition of success change over time?Is there something you haven't done that you still want to?What does a life well-lived look like to you?What would you want written on your tombstone?

This often surfaces unexpected honesty. Be ready to sit with silence.
Chapter 07 · Weeks 26–29

Parenthood & the Children You Raised

The most transformative role most people ever play. These questions invite your subject to reflect on what it meant to be a parent — the love, the fear, the mistakes, and the pride they carry.

Weeks 26 — 29
Week26
What was it like the moment you first held your child — what did you feel that you didn't expect?

What did they look like?What were you afraid of?What did you promise yourself in that moment?Did anything surprise you about the feeling?

Week27
What kind of parent did you want to be — and what kind of parent do you think you were?

What did you consciously try to do differently from your own parents?What did you repeat without meaning to?What are you most proud of as a parent?What would you do differently?

This question often holds the most private feelings parents carry. Approach it gently.
Week28
What is your proudest moment as a parent — and what did it feel like?

Was it a big moment, or something small that nobody else would understand?Did you tell them how proud you were?Is there something you're proud of that they don't know about?

Week29
What do you want your children to know about you that they might not — and what do you want them to know about themselves?

Is there something about your own life they don't fully understand?Is there something you see in them that you've never told them?What do you hope they carry forward from your family?What do you hope they leave behind?

Consider sharing this session's recording directly with the children when it's done.
Chapter 08 · Weeks 30–32

Faith, Belief & the Inner Life

What they believe about life, death, God, and meaning. These questions go beyond religious affiliation to uncover the deepest convictions your subject carries — whether they've spoken them aloud or kept them private for decades.

Weeks 30 — 32
Week30
What did you grow up believing — and how has that changed over time?

What was your family's relationship to faith or religion?Did you embrace it, question it, or leave it behind?Was there a moment that challenged what you believed?What do you believe now that you didn't before?

Week31
What do you believe happens after we die?

Has that belief changed over the course of your life?Does it bring you comfort or something else?What do you hope for?Is there someone who has died that you believe you'll see again?

One of the most profound questions in the whole bank. Don't rush it.
Week32
What is the one thing you believe most deeply — about life, about people, about what matters?

Has life confirmed this belief or tested it?Where did it come from?Is it something you've said out loud, or carried quietly?What would you want your grandchildren to believe?

Chapter 09 · Weeks 33–36

Hardship & Resilience

Every life contains suffering. These questions don't shy away from it — they ask your subject to share the hardest seasons, the losses that changed everything, and what they found inside themselves when things fell apart.

Weeks 33 — 36
Week33
What is the hardest thing you have ever been through?

What was it like at the time?Who was there with you?What got you through it?What did you discover about yourself in that season that you didn't know before?

Be present, not clinical. If they cry, let them. Don't rush to the next question.
Week34
Who have you lost that you still grieve — and what do you miss most about them?

When you think of them, what do you see?What's something about them that only you remember?What would you tell them if you could?How has their absence shaped the person you've become?

Week35
Was there a time in your life when you were genuinely lost — and how did you find your way back?

What did lost feel like for you?Was there a person, a moment, or an idea that helped you turn around?What would you tell someone going through something similar?

Week36
What is the mistake you've made that you've had the hardest time forgiving yourself for?

Have you been able to forgive yourself?Did you make it right? Could you?What would you want someone to know about carrying that kind of weight?

This is deeply personal. Assure them they can share as much or as little as they want.
Chapter 10 · Weeks 37–41

Joy, Gratitude & the Best of It

After the hardship chapters, these sessions invite lightness. What made them laugh, what they savored, the small pleasures and the great ones — the parts of life that made it worth living.

Weeks 37 — 41
Week37
What has made you laugh harder than almost anything in your life?

Tell me the whole story.Who was there?Can you still laugh about it now?What was it about that moment that got you so completely?

Keep the camera rolling no matter what. Laughter is the best thing you'll capture all year.
Week38
What is the most beautiful place you have ever been — and what made it feel that way?

Was it somewhere you traveled, or somewhere close to home?Were you alone or with someone?What did it feel like to be there?Do you ever go back — in memory or in person?

Week39
What are the small, ordinary pleasures of your life that you treasure most?

The morning ritual. The favorite food. The small habit nobody knows about.What does a perfect day look like for you?What do you look forward to every week?What would you miss most if it were gone?

Week40
What are you most grateful for — not in the abstract, but specifically?

Name the specific people, moments, and gifts.Is there something you're grateful for that you never properly thanked someone for?What do you have that you almost didn't?

Week41
What has surprised you most about the life you've lived?

What turned out better than you expected?What didn't?What has life given you that you didn't ask for and turned out to be a gift?What do you know now that would have astonished your younger self?

Chapter 11 · Weeks 42–47

Wisdom & What You Know Now

The distilled knowledge of a lifetime. These questions ask your subject to share what they've learned — about people, about choices, about what matters and what doesn't. The wisdom in these sessions is often what families treasure most.

Weeks 42 — 47
Week42
What is the most important lesson your life has taught you?

Did someone teach it to you, or did you learn it the hard way?When did you know it was true?How would you put it in one sentence for a grandchild?

Week43
What do you know about people now that you wish you'd known at 25?

What took you the longest to understand about human nature?What do you see in people now that you missed when you were young?What kind of person should someone avoid — and what kind should they hold close?

Week44
What do you believe about money — and has that belief served you well?

What did money mean in your family growing up?What did you learn about it the hard way?What would you tell a young person about money that took you decades to understand?What can't money buy that people think it can?

Week45
What do you know about health — the body, the mind — that you wish you'd taken seriously earlier?

What did you take for granted when you were young?What habits do you wish you'd built earlier?What does getting older actually feel like from the inside?What surprised you about aging?

Week46
What do you believe about friendship — what makes a good friend, and what have yours meant to you?

Who is the oldest friend you still have?What is it about a person that makes you trust them?Have you ever had a friendship end — and what did that teach you?What would you want someone to know about being a good friend?

Week47
If you could go back and give your younger self one piece of advice, what would it be?

At what age would you give it?Do you think your younger self would have listened?What do you think would have changed if you had?

The answer to this question is often the most quotable moment in the entire year of sessions.
Chapter 12 · Weeks 48–52

Legacy & What Remains

The final chapter. These questions ask your subject to look at the whole of their life and speak directly to the people who will carry them forward. These sessions often become the most watched of everything you film.

Weeks 48 — 52
Week48
How do you want to be remembered — not by the world, but by the people who loved you?

What do you hope they say about you at your funeral?What do you hope they remember when they think of you years from now?Is there something you want remembered that might be forgotten?

Week49
What do you want your grandchildren — and your grandchildren's children — to know about who you were?

Speak directly to them if you'd like.What do you want them to know about where they came from?What do you hope they inherited from you?What do you hope they do better?

Invite them to speak directly to the camera as if the grandchildren were watching. They often are.
Week50
What is the story from your life that you most want preserved — the one that would be lost without this?

Why this one?Have you ever told it fully before?Who needs to hear it?

Give this session as long as it needs. This is what the whole year has been building toward.
Week51
What has your life been about — what is the through-line, the thread that runs through all of it?

If your life were a story, what would it be about?What were you always moving toward, even when you didn't know it?What are you still becoming?

Week52
Is there anything you've never said that you want to say now — to anyone, about anything?

To a specific person?To the family watching this someday?To yourself?Take as long as you need. We're listening.

The final session. Don't say anything after they finish speaking. Let it land.

You made it.
So did their story.

52 weeks. 52 sessions. A complete portrait of a life — in their own voice, on film, forever. Your family will watch this for generations. Now it's time to edit, organize, and share what you've captured.
