A private YouTube channel is where all your finished episodes live — accessible to every family member, on every device, anywhere in the world, forever. No hard drives to lose. No subscriptions to maintain. This guide shows you how to set one up so only your family can see it.

🌍 Public

Anyone on the internet can find and watch it. Do not use this for family videos — these are private family moments.

🔗 Unlisted Best for most

Only people with the exact link can watch. Not searchable, not findable. Perfect for sharing with family — just send them the link.

🔒 Private

Only specific people you invite by email can watch. The most locked-down option. Slightly more setup, but maximum privacy.

Setup progress — 5 steps
1
Step 1 of 5
Set up a Google account for the family
⏱ About 5 minutes
1
Decide whose account to use

YouTube is owned by Google, so you need a Google account. You have two options: use your existing Gmail account, or — better — create a brand new one just for the family archive, like johnsonfamilystory@gmail.com. A dedicated account keeps the archive separate and easy to hand down to other family members later.

💡
Why a dedicated account is worth it

When you create a family account, you can share the login with siblings, and the archive outlives any one person. If you use your personal Gmail and something happens to you, the family could lose access. A shared family account protects everyone.

2
Create the account (if making a new one)

Go to accounts.google.com/signup. Fill in a name (use the family name, like "Johnson Family"), choose your new email address, and create a password. Write the password down somewhere safe and share it with one trusted family member as a backup.

2
Step 2 of 5
Create your YouTube channel
⏱ About 3 minutes
1
Go to YouTube and sign in

Open your browser and go to youtube.com. Click "Sign in" in the top right corner and sign in with the Google account from Step 1.

2
Click your profile picture, then "Create a channel"

In the top right, click your profile circle (it may just show a letter). A menu drops down. Click "Create a channel".

3
Name your channel and click "Create"

YouTube will ask for a channel name. Use something like "The Johnson Family Story" or "Mom's Life Story". Don't worry about a profile picture for now — you can add one later. Click "Create channel".

ℹ️
The channel name isn't public

Because all your videos will be Unlisted or Private, no stranger will ever see this channel name. It's just for your family's organization. Name it whatever feels right.

3
Step 3 of 5
Upload your first episode
⏱ About 5 minutes + upload time
1
Click the "Create" button and choose "Upload video"

At the top right of YouTube, click the video camera icon with a "+" (the Create button). From the menu, click "Upload video".

📹 Upload video ← Click this
🔴 Go live
📝 Create post
2
Select your edited episode file

Click "Select files" and find your finished, edited episode (the one you exported from CapCut or Descript). It will be in your Downloads, Desktop, or wherever you saved it. Select it and the upload begins automatically.

3
Fill in the title and description while it uploads

While the video uploads in the background, fill in the details:

Title: "Week 1 — Roots & Origins — Mom's Story"
Description: A short note about what this session covered, the date recorded, and anything family members should know.

4
Set "Made for kids" to NO

YouTube will ask "Is this video made for kids?" Select "No, it's not made for kids." This is just a legal question about children's content — it doesn't affect privacy. Your family story is not children's content, so select No.

4
Step 4 of 5 — Most important
Set the privacy to Unlisted or Private
⏱ Don't skip this
⚠️
This is the step that keeps your family videos private

By default, YouTube may try to make your video Public. You must change this. Read this step carefully — it's the difference between a private family archive and putting your loved one's life on the public internet.

1
Continue to the "Visibility" screen

After filling in the details, click "Next" a few times until you reach the "Visibility" screen. This is where you control who can see the video.

2
Choose "Unlisted" (recommended) or "Private"

You'll see three options. Choose Unlisted for the easiest family sharing, or Private for the most locked-down control.

Save or publish — choose who can see your video
Private — Only you and people you choose
Unlisted — Anyone with the link ← Recommended
Public — Everyone (do not choose this)
3
If you chose "Private," add family email addresses

Private videos require you to invite specific people. If you chose Private, a box appears where you type the Gmail addresses of family members who should have access. Note: family members need a Google/Gmail account to watch Private videos. This is why Unlisted is simpler — anyone with the link can watch, no account needed.

4
Click "Save" or "Publish"

Once you've set the visibility, click "Save". Your video is now uploaded and private to your family. YouTube gives you a shareable link — copy it.

5
Step 5 of 5
Organize into a playlist and share
⏱ About 3 minutes
1
Create a playlist for all the episodes

A playlist groups all 52 episodes together in order, so family members can watch the whole story start to finish. Go to your video, click "Save" (under the video), then "Create new playlist." Name it "Mom's Complete Story" and set the playlist privacy to match your videos (Unlisted or Private).

💡
Add every new episode to the same playlist

As you upload each weekly episode, add it to this same playlist. By the end of the year, your family has one link that plays the entire life story in order — like a series they can binge.

2
Share the link with your family

Copy the playlist link and send it to your family by text, email, or a group chat. Here's a message you can use:

"I've started recording Mom's life story — one conversation a week for a year. Here's the private link to watch as new episodes come out. It's just for our family. ❤️ [paste link]"
3
Tell family members how to watch

For Unlisted videos, they just click the link — it opens in any browser or the YouTube app, no account needed. For Private videos, they'll need to sign in with the Gmail address you invited. Remind older family members that they can watch on their phone, tablet, computer, or even their smart TV through the YouTube app.

🎉
Your family archive is live

You now have a permanent, private home for your loved one's story — accessible to your whole family, forever, at no cost. Every week you add an episode, and every family member can watch from anywhere in the world.

Common questions and problems

🔒
"Is Unlisted really private? Can strangers find it?"

Unlisted videos do not appear in search results, on your channel page, or in any recommendations. The only way to watch is to have the exact link. As long as you only share the link with family, no stranger will ever see it. For most families, this is more than private enough.

👵
"My older relatives can't figure out how to watch"

Send them the link and tell them: "Just tap the blue link I sent you — it opens automatically." For Unlisted videos, no sign-in or account is needed. If they have a smart TV, show them the YouTube app once and they can watch on the big screen. Walk them through it on the phone the first time.

⏳
"My video is taking forever to upload"

Upload speed depends on your internet and the video's size. A 20-minute 1080p episode can take 10–30 minutes. Leave the browser tab open and let it finish. If it keeps failing, try uploading when fewer people are using your internet, or move closer to your WiFi router.

📉
"The video looks blurry after uploading"

This is normal for the first few minutes. YouTube processes higher-quality versions after the upload finishes. Wait 15–30 minutes, then refresh — it will sharpen up to full HD. Make sure you exported at 1080p from your editor.

🔑
"What if I lose access to the account?"

This is why we recommend a dedicated family account with the password shared with at least one other trusted family member. Also: always keep your original edited files backed up on your computer and a cloud drive. YouTube is your sharing platform, but your own backup is your true archive.

💾
"Is YouTube a safe place to store these forever?"

YouTube is excellent for sharing and watching, and it's free with unlimited storage. But never rely on it as your only copy. Always keep your edited episodes and raw recordings backed up in two other places — your computer and a cloud service like Google Drive or iCloud, or an external hard drive. YouTube for watching, your own backups for keeping.
