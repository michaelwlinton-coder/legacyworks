Setting up your LegacyWorks Kit

Quick, plain-language setup for each piece of the tested kit — plus a link to the official manufacturer guide for every device. Videos coming soon; for now, these steps get you recording in minutes.

Camera

AnkerWork C310 4K Webcam

▶ Setup video coming soon
  1. Use the included USB 3.0 cable. Plug the USB-C end into the webcam and the USB-A end into your computer. The 4K cable matters — a slower cable won’t give you full quality.
  2. Clip it on top of your monitor or mount it on a small tripod at eye level.
  3. Select it in Riverside. In your recording software, choose “AnkerWork C310” as the camera. Optionally download the free AnkerWork software to fine-tune the picture.
Official AnkerWork setup guide →
Microphone

MAONO HD300T USB/XLR Mic

▶ Setup video coming soon
  1. Assemble the stand. Screw the base, mount, and pop filter together by hand — no tools needed, takes a couple of minutes.
  2. Connect with the USB-C cable (the simplest option). Plug it into your computer — it’s detected automatically as a microphone. Ignore the XLR cable unless you have a mixer.
  3. Select it as your input. In your computer’s sound settings and in Riverside, choose “MAONO HD300T,” then speak within about 6–12 inches for the best sound.
Official MAONO manual & downloads →
Lighting

RALENO 9" LED Light Panel

▶ Setup video coming soon
  1. Charge it fully before your first session using the included USB cable, or leave it plugged in while recording.
  2. Position it behind your camera, facing your subject — slightly above eye level and off to one side gives the most flattering, natural look.
  3. Dial in the light. Use the dials to adjust brightness and warmth until skin tones look natural — not too blue, not too orange. Soft and gentle beats bright and harsh.
Setup is on-device — controls are the two dials on the back. See the printed card in the box.
Connection

USB-C Video Output Hub

▶ Setup video coming soon
  1. Only needed if you’re short on ports. If your computer has enough USB ports for the camera and mic, you may not need the hub at all.
  2. Plug the hub into your computer’s USB-C port, then connect your camera and other devices into the hub.
  3. Confirm everything’s recognized in Riverside before you start — check that both the camera and mic still appear in the device menus.
Plug-and-play — no software or setup required.

Using different gear? The three principles are always the same: plug the camera and mic into your computer, select them in Riverside, and light your subject softly from the front. Every device’s specific buttons are covered in the manual it came with — or search the model name plus “setup” on the manufacturer’s site.
