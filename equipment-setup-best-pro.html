Best Tier Gear
Camera

Logitech Brio 4K Webcam

▶Setup video coming soon
  1. Plug it in with a USB 3.0 port. The Brio is plug-and-play — no drivers needed — but 4K specifically requires a USB 3.0 port (usually the blue one).
  2. Clip it on your monitor or remove the clip to reveal the tripod thread underneath for mounting at eye level.
  3. Select it in Riverside and give it a few seconds to be recognized. Optionally install the free Logi Tune app to adjust field of view, zoom, and lighting.

Microphone

DJI Mic Mini (Wireless)

▶Setup video coming soon
  1. Charge everything in the case first. The transmitter (the clip-on mic) and receiver power on and link automatically the moment you take them out of the charging case.
  2. Clip the transmitter on your subject, about a hand’s width below the chin. Use the magnet or the built-in clip; add the fuzzy windscreen if there’s any breeze.
  3. Connect the receiver to your computer with the USB-C cable, then select “DJI Mic” as the microphone in Riverside. Do a 10-second test recording before the real session.

Lighting

Neewer 2-Pack LED Panels

▶Setup video coming soon
  1. Mount each panel on its stand and power them (battery or the included adapter). With two lights you can shape a much softer, more professional look.
  2. Use the two-light setup: a brighter “key” light at 45° to one side of your subject, and a dimmer “fill” light on the opposite side to soften shadows.
  3. Match the color temperature on both panels (the bi-color dial) so the light looks consistent, and keep both a little above eye level.
On-device dials for brightness & color. See the card in the box for your specific model.
Pro Tier Gear
Camera

Sony ZV-1 II

▶Setup video coming soon
  1. Charge the battery and insert a memory card. For using it as a high-end webcam, you can also power it continuously over USB-C.
  2. To use it as a webcam: connect via USB-C and either use Sony’s free Imaging Edge Webcam software, or a capture device into the HDMI port for the cleanest signal.
  3. Set it on a tripod at eye level, turn on face/eye autofocus, and select the Sony as your camera in Riverside. Frame from the chest up.

Microphone

RØDE Wireless GO II

▶Setup video coming soon
  1. Charge both the transmitter and receiver via USB-C. They come pre-paired and link automatically when powered on.
  2. Clip the transmitter on your subject (it has a built-in mic) or plug in the included lavalier for a more discreet look. Add the fuzzy windscreen if needed.
  3. Connect the receiver to your computer with the USB-C cable — it works as a USB mic directly — then select “RØDE” in Riverside and do a test recording.

Lighting

Godox SL60W + Softbox

▶Setup video coming soon
  1. Mount the light on a sturdy stand and attach the softbox (the Bowens mount twists on). The softbox is what turns a hard light into a gentle, flattering one.
  2. Position it at 45° to your subject, above eye level, angled down slightly — this single softbox alone can light an interview beautifully.
  3. Plug into wall power and adjust brightness on the back dial. Because it’s a mains light, it stays consistent for long sessions.

Joby GorillaPod 3K

▶Setup video coming soon
  1. Attach the ball-head to the flexible legs (if not already attached), then mount your camera onto the quick-release plate on top.

  3. Lock the ball-head once framed, and gently test that it holds your camera’s weight before letting go.
