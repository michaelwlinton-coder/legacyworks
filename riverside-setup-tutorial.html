How to set up
Riverside.fm

Riverside is the software you'll use to record every interview session. This tutorial assumes you have never used it before and have no technical experience. Follow each step exactly and you will be recording within 20 minutes.

✓
No technical skills required
✓
Takes about 20 minutes
✓
Free plan works to start
✓
Works on any computer or phone
Before you start — make sure you have these
  • A computer, tablet, or smartphone (any of these work)
  • An internet connection — WiFi is better than mobile data
  • An email address you can access right now
  • Your subject's email address (you'll invite them at the end)
  • Headphones or earbuds (helps avoid echo) — optional but recommended

Step 4 of 6
Test your microphone and camera
⏱ About 5 minutes — don't skip this
⚠️
Do not skip this step

Testing before your first real session is essential. Nothing is worse than finishing a 45-minute interview and discovering the audio was broken the whole time. Test everything. It takes 5 minutes and could save everything.

1
Enter your studio and click "Start recording"

From your studio dashboard, click "Start recording" or "Go to studio". Before the recording actually starts, Riverside will run you through a quick setup check.

2
Allow Riverside to use your camera and microphone

Your browser will show a small popup asking: "Allow riverside.fm to use your camera and microphone?" Click "Allow". This is normal and safe — Riverside needs access to your camera and mic to record you.

4
Say something out loud and watch the audio bar move

You should see a colored bar or wave that moves when you speak. Say "testing, testing, one two three" out loud. The bar should move. If it moves — your microphone is working. If it doesn't move at all, try a different microphone from the dropdown.

5
Check that you can see yourself on camera

You should see a small video preview of yourself. Make sure your face is visible, the lighting is decent (light should be in front of you, not behind you), and the image is clear. If you just see a black box, your camera may not be turned on — look for a small camera icon on your laptop or check that your webcam is plugged in.

6
Do a 30-second test recording

Click "Start recording" and talk for 30 seconds — say your name, today's date, and count to 10. Then click "Stop recording". Riverside will process it. Then click Play on the recording to make sure you can hear and see yourself clearly. If it looks and sounds good — you are ready.

✅
What "good" sounds like

Your voice should be clear and easy to understand. There shouldn't be a lot of background noise (fans, TV, traffic). Your face should be bright enough to see clearly. If it sounds like you're in a bathroom — you probably are. Hard surfaces create echo. A bedroom or living room with soft furniture is best.

Step 5 of 6
Invite your loved one
⏱ About 3 minutes
ℹ️
Your subject does NOT need a Riverside account

This is the most important thing to tell your parent or grandparent. They don't sign up for anything. They don't download anything. They just click a link you send them, and they're in. It works on any phone, tablet, or computer.

1
Go to your studio dashboard and click "Invite guest"

From inside your Riverside studio, look for a button that says "Invite", "Add guest", or "Share link". Click it.

2
Copy the guest link

Riverside will show you a unique web link — something like riverside.fm/studio/abc123/guest. Click "Copy link" to copy it to your clipboard. This link is how your subject joins the session.

Guest link — share this with your subject
riverside.fm/studio/xk9m2p/guest
Copy link
3
Send the link to your subject by text or email

Paste the link into a text message or email to your loved one. Keep the message simple — here's an example you can copy:

"Hi Mom — for our recording session on [day], just click this link when we're ready to start: [paste link here]. That's all you have to do. It'll open in your browser and you'll see me right away. No sign-up needed."
4
Tell them what to do when they click the link

When your subject clicks the link, their browser will open Riverside and ask them to type their name and allow access to their camera and microphone. They should:

1. Type their first name in the name box
2. Click "Allow" when the browser asks about camera and microphone
3. Click "Join studio" or "Enter"
4. That's it — they'll see your face and you'll see theirs

💡
Do a practice run first

Before your real first session, do a 5-minute practice call with your subject so they're comfortable with the process. Have them click the link and join while you're on the phone with them talking them through it. Then they'll know exactly what to do on session day.

Step 6 of 6
Record your first session
⏱ 30–45 minutes per session
1
Both of you enter the studio

You click "Start studio" from your dashboard. Your subject clicks their guest link. Once you can both see and hear each other clearly, you're ready.

2
Do a quick sound check together

Ask your subject: "Can you hear me clearly?" and "Can you see me?" Ask them to say a few words so you can check their audio. If anything sounds wrong, fix it before recording (see troubleshooting below).

3
Click the red "Record" button to start

When you're both ready, click the red circle button labeled "Record". Riverside will count down — 3, 2, 1 — and then start recording. You'll see a red indicator showing you're live. Begin with a simple opening: "Hi Mom, today is [date] and we're starting our family story project. Let's begin."

4
Ask the question from your 52-week question bank and listen

Open the LegacyWorks question bank to Week 1. Read the question slowly and clearly. Then stop talking — and listen. Your job as an interviewer is to ask questions and listen deeply, not to fill silence. Let them speak. Follow the follow-up prompts when they finish.

5
Click "Stop recording" when the session is done

After 30–45 minutes (or when you've covered the week's question fully), click "Stop recording". Riverside will process the recording — this usually takes a few minutes. Don't close the browser tab while it processes.

6
Download your recording

After processing, you'll see your recording in the studio. Click "Download" to save it to your computer. Save it in a clearly named folder — for example: "Family Story Project / Week 01 — Roots and Origins". Do this after every session without fail.

⚠️
Always download and back up your recordings

Riverside stores your recordings online, but you should always download your own copy. Store it in at least two places — your computer and a cloud service like Google Drive or iCloud. If something ever happens to your computer, your recordings are still safe.

Common problems and how to fix them

🔇
"They can't hear me / I can't hear them"

Fix 1: Check that your speakers or headphones are not muted. Look for a mute button on your keyboard or device. Fix 2: In Riverside, click your name and check the microphone settings — make sure the right mic is selected. Fix 3: Refresh the browser tab and rejoin. That fixes most audio issues instantly.

📷
"The camera isn't working / I just see a black screen"

Fix 1: Check that your camera isn't physically covered — some laptops have a privacy slider over the lens. Fix 2: Close any other apps that might be using your camera (like Zoom or FaceTime) — only one app can use the camera at a time. Fix 3: Refresh the page and click Allow when the browser asks permission again.

🐌
"The video is choppy / freezing"

Fix 1: Move closer to your WiFi router — distance weakens the connection. Fix 2: Ask your subject to do the same on their end. Fix 3: Close other browser tabs and apps on your computer — they compete for internet. Fix 4: Don't worry — Riverside records each person locally on their own device, so even if the video call is choppy, the actual recording quality is fine.

🔊
"There's a terrible echo / feedback noise"

This almost always means someone's speakers are picking up the other person's voice and creating a loop. Fix: Both of you should use headphones or earbuds. This is the single most effective thing you can do for audio quality.

🚫
"My subject can't figure out how to click the link"

Stay on the phone with them while they open it. Tell them exactly what to click step by step: "Open Safari / Chrome / whatever you use" → "Type the link I sent you" → "Click Allow" → "Type your name" → "Click Join." If they still can't get in, have them call you back in 5 minutes while they try again. Patience and a phone call fixes this every time.

📁
"I can't find my recording after it finished"

Go back to your Riverside studio dashboard — the main screen when you log in. Your recordings are listed under your studio name. Click on the studio, find the recording, and download it. If you don't see it, wait 10 minutes and refresh — Riverside sometimes takes a few minutes to process recordings.

💳
"Riverside is asking me to upgrade / pay"

The free plan gives you 2 hours of recording per month, which is enough for 2–3 sessions. If you hit the limit, you have two options: Option 1 — Upgrade to Riverside Standard ($19/month) to get unlimited recording. Option 2 — Wait until the next calendar month when your free hours reset. We recommend upgrading — at $19/month for the year you're recording, it's worth it.

Before every session — your quick checklist

Environment: I'm in a quiet room with soft furnishings (bedroom, living room — not a kitchen or bathroom)
Lighting: Light is in front of my face, not behind me — no windows behind me
Earbuds/headphones: Both of us are using them to avoid echo
Phone on silent: Both phones are on silent or Do Not Disturb
Question ready: I've read this week's question from the LegacyWorks question bank
Test recording done: I've done a 30-second test and confirmed audio and video are working
Subject is comfortable: They're seated comfortably, have water nearby, know they can take breaks
Ready to listen: I'm not going to check my phone or interrupt — just ask, and listen
